Satoru
Iwata

December 6, 1959 - July 11, 2015

Satoru Iwata was a talented Japanese game developer and president of Nintendo. Here is a brief timeline of his life in the video game industry.

  • 1959Satoru Iwata was born on December 6, 1959, and raised in Sapporo, Japan.
  • 1975He entered the Hokkaido Sapporo South High School and began developing games in his junior year.
  • 1978Iwata was admitted to the Tokyo Institute of Technology and majored in computer science.
  • 1980He was employed as a part-time programmer at HAL Laboratory while attending college.
  • 1982After completing his studies, Iwata joined HAL Laboratory as a full-time employee.
  • 1983He became coordinator of software production at HAL and spearheaded the company’s efforts to develop games for Nintendo’s NES game console.
  • 1993Iwata was promoted to president of HAL. He would lift the company from the brink of bankruptcy and stabilize its finalcials within six years with Nintendo’s support.
  • 2000Iwata joined Nintendo as the head of corporate planning. He also had a seat on the board of directors.
  • 2004He became Nintendo’s fourth president and introduced a blue ocean strategy that would result in the release of the Nintendo DS and later the Wii. Both devices aimed to provide a unique gameplay experience to a wider audience over the typical approach of increased fidelity in games.
  • 2005Iwata revealed the Wii, Nintendo’s next game console, at E3. Later in 2005 at the Tokyo Game show he revealed it’s tv remote like controller.
  • 2006He launched Iwata Asks, a series of articles where he interviewed Nintendo developers about their games.
  • 2011Iwata helped introduce Nintendo direct, the company’s live virtual event that was open to all worldwide via online streaming platforms like Youtube.
  • 2012Iwata revealed the Wii, Nintendo’s next game console, at E3. Later in 2005 at the Tokyo Game show he revealed it’s strange controller.
  • 2014In January 2014 Iwata along with other high ranking Nintendo execs took drastic paycuts in respose to the Wii U’s poor sales figures. He didn’t attend the E3 2014 conference due to his surgery done a week prior. He did however appear in an epic pre-recorded fight sequence with Reggie Fils-Aimé.
  • 2015Iwata was hospitalized due to serious illness and died on July 11 at the age of 55.
  • 2017Nintendo released the Nintendo Switch, a new hybrid console, worldwide on March 3, 2017. It was a part of a strategy initiated by Iwata and a few other Nintendo execs to reinvigorate Nintendo’s sales after the lacluster sales of the Wii U. It was an evolution of their blue ocean strategy that spawned the Wii and sought to differenciate Nintendo’s console offering with a unique experience. The switch has been a massive success and is my favourite game console of all time. Thank you Iwata.
